# 🤖 AI Impact on Jobs & Layoff Risk Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of the **AI Impact on Jobs and Layoff Risk** dataset. The objective is to understand how Artificial Intelligence (AI) adoption, workplace automation, employee skills, and workforce characteristics influence layoff risk across different industries.

The analysis explores patterns in AI adoption, automation exposure, workforce adaptability, and employment stability to identify the key factors associated with employee resilience in an AI-driven economy.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement

Artificial Intelligence is rapidly transforming workplaces by automating routine tasks and changing workforce requirements. While AI enhances productivity, it also introduces concerns about job displacement. This project investigates how AI adoption, automation exposure, employee characteristics, and skill requirements relate to layoff risk.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze workforce demographics and professional characteristics.
- Study AI adoption across industries and job roles.
- Identify factors contributing to employee layoff risk.
- Explore workforce adaptability and automation exposure.
- Generate business insights to understand employment resilience in an AI-driven workplace.

---

## 📂 Dataset

- **Source:** Kaggle
- **Dataset:** AI Impact on Jobs and Layoff Risk Dataset
- **Records:** 20,000 synthetic employee records
- **Features:** Demographics, job characteristics, AI adoption, automation metrics, and layoff risk.

---

## 🔍 Research Questions

- Which industries are most vulnerable to AI-driven layoffs?
- How does AI adoption differ across industries and company sizes?
- Which workforce characteristics are associated with high layoff risk?
- Does creativity reduce automation exposure?
- Which employee groups are most resilient to AI-driven automation?
- What factors contribute most to layoff risk?

## 📈 Key Insights

- Employees with higher automation exposure tend to have higher layoff risk.
- Jobs requiring greater creativity and human interaction are generally less vulnerable to automation.
- Workforce adaptability is associated with greater employment resilience.
- AI adoption and automation vary across industries.
- Continuous upskilling and AI training are essential for adapting to an AI-driven workplace.

---

## 📌 Conclusion

The analysis highlights that AI adoption and automation have a significant association with workforce employment risk. Employees engaged in highly routine and automatable tasks are more likely to face higher layoff risk, whereas creativity, human interaction, adaptability, and continuous learning contribute to greater employment resilience. These findings can help organizations develop strategies for workforce upskilling and AI readiness.
